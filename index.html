The Selkie Inne Story

Selkie — in Irish and Scottish folklore, a seal who sheds her skin to walk on land. Gentle. Magnetic. A healer by nature.

Inne — Irish Gaelic for "island." A place apart, surrounded by water. A sanctuary by the sea.

Together: the selkie's island. A safe, healing place where the boundaries between worlds grow soft.

I move between modalities like a selkie between worlds — from deep lymphatic drainage to corrective movement to Reiki energy work. Each session is an act of translation, meeting you exactly where your body is, honoring the lived experience of hypermobility and the complex conditions that often accompany it.

Rooted in Irish and Scottish mythology, coastal in spirit, Selkie Inne is a practice built for bodies that have often been misunderstood — and for the people who inhabit them.

About Me

I'm Nicole Tackaberry — a Licensed Massage Therapist, Manual Lymphatic Drainage specialist, and Corrective Exercise Specialist with a deep focus on Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome, hypermobility, and complex conditions. My integrative approach honors the full picture — body, nervous system, and lived experience — creating a safe space for people who have often been dismissed or misunderstood in traditional healthcare settings.

My clients often come with overlapping issues: MCAS, POTS, dysautonomia, chronic pain, autism, trauma, and a history of being dismissed by other healthcare providers. I treat the whole person, not just the symptom. I listen. I understand. And I know how to work with sensitive, hypermobile bodies in ways that honor their fragility and their strength.

Each session is designed to reduce nervous system dysregulation, improve lymphatic flow, correct dysfunctional movement patterns, and restore a sense of safety in your own body.
